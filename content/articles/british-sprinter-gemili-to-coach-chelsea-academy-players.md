**Adam Gemili, the British Olympic sprinter, has announced his retirement from professional athletics and will be joining Chelsea academy as a speed coach.**

## Context / Background
Adam Gemili's athletic career has been nothing short of remarkable. He transitioned from playing for Dagenham and Redbridge academy to becoming the World Junior 100m champion and participating in the London 2012 Olympics.

## Tactical Key Points
Gemili's experience and expertise in sprinting will undoubtedly bring a new dimension to Chelsea's academy. His understanding of speed and agility will help young players enhance their performance on the field.

## Key Quote
> Although there are no direct quotes available from Adam Gemili, his decision to join Chelsea academy as a speed coach speaks volumes about his passion for the sport and his desire to give back to the community.

## What's Next?
As Gemili begins his new role, he will be working closely with the academy players to improve their speed and agility. This partnership is expected to yield positive results for Chelsea's young talents.
