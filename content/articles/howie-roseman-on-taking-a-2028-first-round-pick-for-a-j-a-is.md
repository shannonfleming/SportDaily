**Executive Summary**: The Philadelphia Eagles have agreed to trade wide receiver A.J. Brown to the New England Patriots in exchange for two draft picks, including a 2028 first-round pick. Eagles general manager Howie Roseman commented on the trade, stating that a draft pick is a valuable asset.

## Context / Background
The trade comes as a surprise to many, given Brown's production on the field. However, the Eagles' decision to trade him may be due to various factors, including salary cap considerations and the team's overall roster construction.

| Patriots | A.J. Brown | WR | 2 draft picks |

> A pick is a pick, and we'll take it, said Howie Roseman, Eagles general manager, when asked about the trade.

## What's Next?
The trade is expected to have significant implications for both teams. The Patriots will be gaining a talented wide receiver, while the Eagles will be looking to rebuild their roster with the acquired draft picks.
