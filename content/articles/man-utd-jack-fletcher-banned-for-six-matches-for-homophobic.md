**Manchester United's Jack Fletcher has been banned for six matches after using homophobic language towards an opponent.**
## Context / Background
The incident occurred during a recent match, and an investigation was launched by the Football Association (FA). The FA has a zero-tolerance policy towards homophobic abuse, and Fletcher's actions were deemed to be in breach of this policy.

Fletcher has apologized for his actions, stating that he realizes his mistake and will work to regain the trust of his teammates and fans.
> I want to apologize for my actions, I realize that my words were unacceptable and I will do my best to make sure it doesn't happen again.
## What's Next?
Fletcher will miss the next six matches for Manchester United, which could have a significant impact on the team's performance. The club has stated that they will provide support and guidance to Fletcher to help him understand the severity of his actions.
