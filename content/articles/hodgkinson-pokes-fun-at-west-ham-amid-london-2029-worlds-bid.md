**Keely Hodgkinson has poked fun at West Ham amid the dispute over the London Stadium's potential hosting of the 2029 World Athletics Championships.** 
## Context / Background
The London Stadium, which was built for the 2012 Olympics, is at the center of a dispute between West Ham and UK Athletics over its potential use for the 2029 World Athletics Championships. West Ham, the current tenants of the stadium, have expressed concerns about the impact of athletics events on the stadium's condition and their own schedule.

Keely Hodgkinson, the 800m Olympic champion, has thrown her support behind the bid to host the championships at the London Stadium, mocking West Ham's lack of 'medals' in the process.
> 'I think it's funny that West Ham are worried about the stadium when they haven't won many medals themselves,' Hodgkinson joked.
## What's Next?
The bidding process for the 2029 World Athletics Championships is ongoing, with a decision expected to be made in the coming months. If London is successful, it will be a major boost to the city's sporting credentials and provide a unique opportunity for British athletes to compete on the world stage.
