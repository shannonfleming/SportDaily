**A bid to host the Olympic and Paralympic Games in the North of England in the 2040s is being planned by the Government after it authorised UK Sport to begin a feasibility study.**
## Context / Background
The UK Government has given the green light for a feasibility study to assess the possibility of hosting the Olympic and Paralympic Games in the North of England in the 2040s. This 'multi-city' bid is an exciting prospect for the region, which could potentially bring significant investment and infrastructure development.

## Tactical Key Points / Stats Table
The feasibility study will consider various factors, including the availability of suitable venues, transportation links, and accommodation options. While no specific details have been released, it is likely that the study will examine the potential for a dispersed event, with different sports and events taking place across multiple cities in the North of England.

> A bid to host the Olympic and Paralympic Games in the North of England would be a significant undertaking, requiring careful planning and coordination between various stakeholders, including local authorities, sports organizations, and government agencies.

## What's Next?
The feasibility study is expected to be completed in the coming months, after which a decision will be made on whether to proceed with a formal bid. If successful, the North of England could become a major hub for international sports events, bringing economic benefits and promoting the region's profile on the global stage.
