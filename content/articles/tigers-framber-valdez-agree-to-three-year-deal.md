## Tigers Land Top Free Agent: Framber Valdez Signs Three-Year Deal
The Detroit Tigers have made a significant splash in the free agent market, agreeing to a three-year deal with left-handed pitcher Framber Valdez. The deal is reportedly worth $115 million, making it one of the largest contracts in Tigers history.

## How Framber Valdez Became the Tigers' Top Target
The Tigers had been pursuing Valdez for several weeks, and it's clear why he was their top target. The 29-year-old pitcher has established himself as one of the top left-handers in the American League, with a strong track record of success. In 2022, Valdez went 17-6 with a 2.82 ERA, striking out 194 batters in 201.1 innings. He has been one of the most consistent pitchers in the league, with a career ERA of 3.35 and a WHIP of 1.23.

## What Valdez's Signing Means for the Tigers
The signing of Valdez is a significant boost to the Tigers' rotation, which was in need of a top-of-the-line starter. Valdez will join a rotation that includes Tarik Skubal, Matt Manning, and Joey Wentz, giving the Tigers a strong foundation for the 2024 season. The Tigers are hoping that Valdez will be the anchor of their rotation, providing a consistent and dominant presence on the mound.

## Reactions from Around the League
The signing of Valdez has sent shockwaves around the league, with many teams and players reacting to the news. Tarik Skubal, the Tigers' young left-hander, tweeted a message of congratulations to Valdez, saying "Welcome to the team, Framber! Can't wait to learn from you and throw alongside you." The Orioles, who were also rumored to be interested in Valdez, are reportedly pivoting to other free agent targets.
