**The Chicago Bears are one step closer to relocating to Indiana after the team's board of directors voted to advance the development of a new stadium in Hammond.**
## Context / Background
The Bears have been exploring options for a new stadium, and the vote marks a significant milestone in the team's potential relocation to Indiana. The team has been playing at Soldier Field in Chicago since 1971, but the stadium has undergone several renovations and expansions over the years.
## Tactical Key Points / Stats Table
The proposed stadium in Hammond would be a state-of-the-art facility, with a seating capacity of over 60,000. The team has not released any official details on the stadium's design or construction timeline, but it is expected to be a major development project for the region.
