    **The New York Mets have made a significant change in their management, firing manager Carlos Mendoza after a disappointing start to the season.**
    
    ## Context and Background
    The Mets' decision to fire Carlos Mendoza comes after the team posted a 34-47 record, which has been underwhelming for a team with high expectations. The first half of the season has been marred by inconsistency and lack of cohesion, leading to the front office's decision to make a change.
    
    ## Tactical Key Points
    The Mets' struggles can be attributed to various factors, including inconsistent pitching, lack of offense, and poor defensive play. The team's inability to close out games and their struggles against divisional opponents have been particularly concerning.
    
    > The Mets' front office will be looking for a new manager who can turn the team's fortunes around and make a push for the playoffs in the second half of the season.
    
    ## What's Next?
    The Mets have appointed Andy Green as the interim manager, and the team will be looking to bounce back from their disappointing start. The second half of the season will be crucial for the Mets, and they will need to show significant improvement if they want to make a push for the playoffs.
