**The International Olympic Committee (IOC) has announced a new policy banning transgender women athletes from competing in female events at the Olympics, starting from the 2028 Olympics.**

## Context / Background
The IOC's decision comes after a thorough review of the current policies and guidelines regarding the participation of transgender athletes in Olympic competitions. The new policy aims to protect the female category in Olympic sports and ensure fair competition.

## Tactical Key Points / Stats Table
The IOC's policy change is based on the idea that transgender women athletes may have a competitive advantage over cisgender women athletes due to their physical characteristics. The policy states that athletes who have transitioned from male to female will not be eligible to compete in female events, unless they meet certain criteria.

> The IOC's decision has sparked controversy and debate, with some arguing that it is discriminatory and unfair to transgender athletes.

## What's Next?
The new policy will come into effect at the 2028 Olympics, and athletes who wish to compete in female events will need to meet the IOC's eligibility criteria. The IOC has stated that it will continue to review and update its policies to ensure that they are fair and inclusive for all athletes.
