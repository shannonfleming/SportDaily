Tennis legend Raphael Nadal has led a chorus of support for American skier Lindsey Vonn, who was airlifted to hospital after a horror crash in the women's downhill event at the Milano Cortina 2026 Winter Olympics. Vonn, a four-time World Cup champion and Olympic gold medalist, suffered a severe crash while competing with a torn ACL, sparking widespread concern among the sports community.

## The Crash and Its Aftermath
The incident occurred during the women's downhill event at the Winter Olympics, where Vonn lost control and crashed into a fence. The American skier was treated on the slopes before being airlifted to hospital for further treatment. The crash has sent shockwaves through the sports world, with many athletes and fans taking to social media to express their support for Vonn. Nadal, a long-time admirer of Vonn's bravery and skill on the slopes, was among the first to offer his support, tweeting "Get well soon, Lindsey!
