## Frequently Asked Questions
Q: What is a ruptured ACL, and how severe is the injury?
A: A ruptured ACL is a serious injury that occurs when the anterior cruciate ligament, which connects the femur to the tibia, is torn. It can be extremely painful and debilitating, requiring surgical intervention and extensive rehabilitation.
Q: How can Lindsey Vonn ski with a ruptured ACL?
A: Vonn can ski with a ruptured ACL due to the unique nature of downhill skiing, which requires less lateral movement and quick changes of direction compared to other sports. Her specialized equipment, such as ski boots and bindings, also provides additional support and stability.
