**Trump has signed an executive order aimed at regulating college sports, including transfer limits, in a move that could significantly impact the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) and its policies.**

## Context / Background
The executive order is intended to stabilize college sports and threatens lost federal funding if the NCAA does not comply. This move comes after months of debate about the role of the NCAA in regulating college athletics and the rights of student-athletes.

## Tactical Key Points / Stats Table
| Category | New Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Transfer Limits | 1 transfer per athlete |
| Eligibility | 5 years of eligibility |
The new rules aim to limit NCAA athletes to 5 years of eligibility and 1 transfer, which could have significant implications for college sports.

> The executive order is a significant development in the ongoing debate about the role of the NCAA in regulating college athletics.

## What's Next?
The NCAA and college sports conferences will need to respond to the executive order and determine how to implement the new rules. This could involve changes to existing policies and procedures, as well as potential legal challenges.
