**The English Football League (EFL) has announced a significant change to the Sky Bet Championship play-off format, expanding the competition to six teams from the 2026/27 season onwards.**
## Context / Background
The EFL Championship play-offs have traditionally featured four teams, with the third to sixth-placed teams competing for a spot in the Premier League. However, following approval from EFL clubs, the format will now include six teams, with the third to eighth-placed teams participating in the play-offs.

## Tactical Key Points / Stats Table
The new format is expected to increase competition and provide more teams with a chance to earn promotion to the Premier League. The teams that finish between third and eighth place will compete in a series of matches, with the winners earning a spot in the Premier League.

> The expansion of the play-offs is a positive step for the EFL, providing more teams with a chance to compete for a spot in the Premier League.

## What's Next?
The new format will come into effect from the 2026/27 season, with the exact details of the competition structure and schedule to be announced in the coming months.
