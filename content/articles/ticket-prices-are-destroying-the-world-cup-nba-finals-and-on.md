**The rising cost of sports tickets is a growing concern for fans worldwide, with many feeling priced out of the market.** 
## Context and Background
The sports industry has witnessed a significant surge in ticket prices over the past few years, with the World Cup, NBA Finals, and Stanley Cup being among the most affected. This trend has led to a decline in attendance and viewership, as fans are no longer able to afford the high costs of tickets.
## Tactical Key Points and Stats
| Event | Average Ticket Price |
| --- | --- |
| World Cup | $150-$300 |
| NBA Finals | $100-$200 |
| Stanley Cup | $80-$150 |
The increasing ticket prices have resulted in a decrease in demand, with many fans opting for alternative ways to watch their favorite teams and players.
> The price of watching live sport in the U.S. has ballooned — and the World Cup is taking full advantage of the landscape. In chasing maximum revenue, FIFA risks shrinking the very audience that made the World Cup seemingly priceless.
## What's Next?
As the sports industry continues to grapple with the issue of rising ticket prices, fans can expect to see a shift in the way teams and leagues approach ticket sales. With the rise of online streaming and social media, fans may have more options to engage with their favorite sports without having to break the bank.
