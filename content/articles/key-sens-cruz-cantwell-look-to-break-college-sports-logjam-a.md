**The US Senate has introduced a bipartisan bill to reform college sports, aiming to address issues such as player compensation, transfer rules, and coach poaching.**
## Context / Background
The bill, proposed by Senators Cruz and Cantwell, seeks to create a more equitable and sustainable system for college athletes. The current system has faced criticism for its treatment of athletes, with many arguing that they should be allowed to profit from their own name, image, and likeness.
## Tactical Key Points / Stats Table
* The bill proposes a salary cap for college athletes
* It also limits the number of transfers allowed per athlete
* The bill aims to increase transparency and accountability in college sports
> The bipartisan bill is a significant step towards reforming the college sports system, providing a more level playing field for athletes and promoting fairness and integrity in the sport.
## What's Next?
The bill will now be reviewed and debated by the Senate, with the potential to become law in the coming months. If passed, it could have a significant impact on the college sports landscape, affecting thousands of athletes and institutions across the US.
