**President Trump has signed an executive order intended to stabilize college sports, with a focus on limiting NCAA athletes to 5 years of eligibility and 1 transfer.**
## Context / Background
The order is part of a broader effort to reform college sports, with the President threatening to withhold federal funding from schools that do not comply. This move has sparked debate among college sports administrators, athletes, and fans.
## Tactical Key Points / Stats Table
| Category | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Eligibility | 5 years of eligibility for NCAA athletes |
| Transfers | 1 transfer allowed per athlete |
| Funding | Federal funding at risk for non-compliant schools |
## Key Quote
> 'The executive order aims to promote fairness and transparency in college sports, while also protecting the rights of student-athletes' - Source: White House.
## What's Next?
The order is expected to have far-reaching implications for college sports, with schools and athletes alike waiting to see how the reforms will be implemented. The NCAA has yet to comment on the order, but is expected to release a statement in the coming days.
