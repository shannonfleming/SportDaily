**The National Basketball Association (NBA) has taken a strong stance against tanking, fining the Utah Jazz $500,000 and the Indiana Pacers $100,000 for violating the league's player participation policy.**
## Context / Background
The NBA has been dealing with the issue of tanking, where teams intentionally lose games to improve their chances of getting a higher draft pick. The league has been trying to prevent this practice, which is seen as detrimental to the integrity of the game.
## Tactical Key Points / Stats Table
| Team | Fine Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Utah Jazz | $500,000 |
| Indiana Pacers | $100,000 |
The fines imposed on the Jazz and Pacers are a clear indication that the NBA is serious about preventing tanking. The league wants to ensure that all teams are competing at their best and that the outcome of games is not predetermined.
> The NBA's decision to fine the Jazz and Pacers is a step in the right direction, as it shows that the league is committed to maintaining the integrity of the game.
## What's Next?
The NBA will continue to monitor the situation and take action against any team that is found to be tanking. The league will also review its policies and procedures to ensure that they are effective in preventing tanking.
