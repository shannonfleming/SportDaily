**The WNBA has reached a new collective bargaining agreement (CBA) that significantly increases player salaries and gives them more control over their careers.**
## Context / Background
The new CBA is a major step forward for the WNBA, with the salary cap set to top $10M by the end of the agreement. This increase in pay is a significant win for players, who have been fighting for better compensation for years.

## Tactical Key Points / Stats Table
| Category | Old CBA | New CBA |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Salary Cap | $1.4M | $10M |
| Player Salary | $40,000 - $100,000 | $60,000 - $200,000 |
| Revenue Sharing | 20% | 30% |

> The new CBA is a testament to the strength and unity of the WNBA players, who have fought tirelessly for fair compensation and better working conditions.

## What's Next?
The new CBA is set to take effect immediately, with the 2026 season being the first to be played under the new agreement. Fans can expect to see increased competition and excitement on the court, as players are now more invested in the league than ever before.
