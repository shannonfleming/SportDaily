**The England Lionesses are left to ponder what could have been after being handed the toughest group in World Cup qualifying, with their hopes of automatic qualification now hanging in the balance.**

## Context / Background
The England women's national team, led by Sarina Wiegman, won five of their six qualifying matches but still found themselves facing an uphill battle for a spot in next year's World Cup. The team's disappointment stems from the fact that one defeat, particularly to Spain, has put their qualification in jeopardy.

Their only loss in the group stage was to Spain, who topped the group with a perfect record. This defeat has raised questions among the team and its supporters about the fairness of the qualifying system.

> The Lionesses' situation serves as a reminder of the competitiveness and challenges faced by teams in international football, where a single loss can have significant implications for their chances of advancing to major tournaments.

## What's Next?
The Lionesses will now have to wait and see how the rest of the qualifying process unfolds, potentially facing a playoff to secure their spot in the World Cup. The team remains hopeful that they can still achieve their goal of participating in the tournament, despite the current setback.
