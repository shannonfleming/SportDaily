    **Aintree winner has been disqualified and the jockey banned for 28 days after exceeding the whip limit by four strikes.**
    ## Context / Background
    The incident occurred during a recent race at Aintree, where the jockey was found to have breached the whip limit. The British Horseracing Authority (BHA) has strict rules in place regarding the use of whips in horse racing, and exceeding the limit can result in penalties for the jockey.
    ## Tactical Key Points / Stats Table
    | Penalty | Duration |
    | --- | --- |
    | Whip limit breach | 28-day ban |
    The jockey's actions were deemed to have compromised the safety and welfare of the horse, leading to the disqualification and ban.
    > The BHA takes whip limit breaches very seriously, and this incident highlights the importance of adhering to the rules and prioritizing horse welfare.
    ## What's Next?
    The jockey will serve a 28-day ban, effective immediately. The disqualified horse will be replaced by the runner-up in the official results.
